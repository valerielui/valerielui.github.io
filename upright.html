about Upright

Upright is a food and beverage startup selling the first allergen-free, high-protein instant oatmilk.

what I did

During my internship, I planned and executed a variety of marketing tasks to facilitate Upright's growth and reach of more customers. My main task was social media content creation and management, where I developed a content strategy that included the introduction of two content series: Jessie’s Recipe of the Week and an informational infographic series. As part of the recipe series, I also launched Upright’s blog and featured weekly recipes on blog posts as well. In setting up the blog, I also worked on search engine optimization, and successfully placed Upright’s main website and product listing page within the top three search results.

Over the summer, I also worked on creating a referral giveaway program for Upright. As part of this project, I helped coordinate the marketing efforts of the giveaway, which included creating various forms of content, as well as launching advertising campaigns on both Instagram and Facebook to reach a larger audience. Additionally, I also designed and developed a landing page that detailed and consolidated all information about the giveaway. The giveaway ultimately was extremely successful, as we generated over 1000+ leads through the giveaway within a month. This program, combined with other social media marketing efforts, also increased Upright’s social media following and reach by over 7,714% and 3,700% respectively within a year.

Towards the end of my internship, I hosted a few giveaways on smaller scales and have been working on a revamp of Upright’s Instagram brand as well as incorporating more forms of content — especially Reels and stories — into the account.

what I have learned

how to create content with an audience in mind
As part of my role, I had to create a lot of customer-facing creative work that required skills like graphic and web design - which I already had. However, initially I had trouble grasping the types of content that I should be putting out to maximize engagement. It took a bit of trial and error, reflecting on previous work, and a lot of planning and strategizing to figure out what worked best in helping us engage our audience.

a LOT of new skills
Throughout the internship, I had ideas for many types of content outside of graphics — which I had the most experience with. For example, I wanted to create videos with sound waves for podcast episodes, short-form Instagram Reels videos, and more! Therefore, in order to turn these ideas into reality, I took it upon myself to teach myself a few new programs like Adobe Premiere Pro, After Effects, and also Reels! Besides the learning curve, it took a lot of trial and error to figure out how I can best utilize these different forms of content to engage as well as grow our audience. Ultimately, the content I’ve created with these new skills turned out to be some of my favorite posts I’ve put out for Upright!

a more systematic work approach
In this role, I had a lot of creative freedom, which at times made it hard to navigate the ambiguity especially with tasks I was unfamiliar with. Like with social media, it took me months to find a direction I could go in to make our content engaging in different ways while also ensuring our feed looks cohesive. Therefore, I’ve really learned to become more organized and systematic in terms of approaching slightly ambiguous tasks like this. With social media again, how I managed this task was by first taking time to find inspiration on other accounts. Then, I would compile them and think about how I could create content based on the same idea to engage my audience before finally going into designing and organizing it in my feed and posting.
